Dense Passage Retrieval (DPR) is deep learning approaches applied to information retrieval problems where the encoder part of LLMs (e.g. BERT) are utilized to encode query and short text documents in a shared latent vector space and then ranked based on their proximity. DPR models are trained based on some ranking/ retrieval objective. However, in this work, we show that for certain types of retrieval scenarios (overview passage retrieval), these models can benefit from an additional clustering objective that incorporates query-specific subtopic information.
